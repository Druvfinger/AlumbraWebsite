FAQ

Material and material impact

Which materials are suitable for aluminium dip brazing?

Not all aluminium alloys are suitable. A high alloy content of certain materials can limit it's solderability. The alloy composition should me of the type AlMgSi. for example EN-AW 6082 and EN-AW 6083, but pure aluminium also works without issue.

How is the material impacted by the process?

The strength of the core material is affected by the brazing process. Heating the material to close to the melting point results in that the material taking on the solidity of the corresponding annealed state. After a couple of days spent at room temperature, curable materials will regain a part of their solidity.

To further increase the strength of the curable alloys the material can be heat treated and aged.

The dip brazing technology

What is the salt bath composed of?

The salt bath is composed of potassium, sodium and lithium chlorides aswell as a small amount of diffrent fluorides.

The salt is heated with the help of electrodes to approx. 595 degrees C while brazing. The salt melt acts both as a heatsource and as flux in the brazing process.

How big can the components be?

The salt baths soldering oven(reflow oven??). Alumbra has two ovens. Their measurments are: 1000x 1000 x 800mm and 1200x800x800 mm.

The disposable brazing space is slightly smaller since the brazing arrangement takes up some space.

Is further tools or fixtures required?

By designing the components so that they are self-fixing, this allows the need for fixtures to be avoided. This has multiple benefits:

• Cost of construction and manufacturing of fixtures can be avoided.
• Maintenance of fixtures after wear and tear are not necessary and if the component undergoes reconstruction new ones do not have to be made.
• Heating and cooling times of heavy fixtures are excluded which reduces the processing time
• Multiple components can fit in one brazing batch.
• Lower weight and therefore easier handling of a brazing batch.

Sometimes fixation aids or fixtures are necessary. These are made of aluminium alloys or stainless steel. To avoid the joining of aluminium fixtures to the parts that are supposed to be joined they are anodized. The variations and possibilities are plentiful.

What is the solder material made of?

The solder material is made up of aluminium with a high silicon content. Roughly 12% Silicon is used to lower the solders melting point. Solder exists in the form of thread and foil in different dimensions and thicknesses as well as in powder form. Solder plated aluminium plates of different thicknesses are also available.

When the solder melts and get pulled in to the joint by the capillary force and pushes the salt out, some of the material difueses slightly between base material and solder. A certain exchange of material will happen and the joined components will bind together with a strong joint. The solder is not curable and effect of the heat treatment is not significant enough to be worth mentioning. The tensile strength,Rm(????), of the solder is between 100-110 N/mm².

Are the materials corrosion properties effected?

The salt is very corrosive but completely water soluble. To avoid corrosion issues all the salt remains are removed through careful cleaning in hot water in multiple steps. The method therefore does not affect the corrosion properties of the material.

The solder has a good corrosion resistance since its corrosion potential is close to that of AlMgSi and is why the risk of galvanic corrosion does not exist.

What level of precision and tolerance does the method provide?

Before brazing, the components that are going to be joined need to be cleaned to remove dirt and grease as well as breaking down the oxide layer that covers the components. This is done through degreasing and pickling in sulfuric acid and nitric acid. This chemical pickling and the salt baths fluxing properties do to some degree effect the dimension of the starting material, but the effect is marginal (max 0.02 mm)

After brazing the parts are cooled in air. Depending on the design of the construction and the composition of the components, the cooling process will take more or less time. This can in some cases lead to some shape change. These changes can generally be adjusted after brazing.

The level of measurement accuracy that can be achieved with this method is dependent on the design of the part as well as how many joints can affect the desired measurements accuracy. The following example gives an indication of the method's potential accuracy. Suppose we join a box with 4 walls and a bottom with dip-brazing. Dimensions are 100x100x100 mm. A result with a measurement accuracy of within a couple of tenths of a mm in width and height is what we can expect. Generally speaking measurement requirements down to 0.2-0.3 mm can be placed on sheet metal construction whilst measurements down to 0.05 can be handled in constructions based off of cutting processes.

Is the surface treatment possibilities effected by the brazing process?

The possibility to use different surface treatment options is not in any way effected by the brazing process. If you want decorative anodizing some patchiness can occur in and around the joint. This is because the increased silicon content of solder means that the joint will have a slightly darker tone than the base material.
